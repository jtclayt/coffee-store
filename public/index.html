About Our Company

PF roasting originally started as a food service business in 2008 selling corn, potatoes, turkey legs, and other fair foods at festivals and rodeos around Washington state. It was founded by my dad who enjoyed traveling and cooking food. After I joined the Marine corps in 2010 he continued to do food services for a time but eventually sold off the equipment for the company.

While I was stationed as a security guard at the US Embassy in Kinshasa, Democratic Republic of the Congo I began roasting coffee as a way to supply myself coffee at a cheaper price. I started roasting with just a bag of green beans and frying pan. I quickly discovered enjoyment in this newfound hobby and would spend the next 3 years travelling across the globe to various posts taking my coffee roasting supplies with me.

Every post I was stationed at I would try to provide my coffee to my fellow marines and tell them of the quality and flavor of fresh roasted beans. At this point I had roasted beans from every coffee proucing country in the world and had used various methods to roast them.

In 2016, I returned home from the Marine Corps and I convinced my dad to take up the hobby of roasting coffee and we began investing in better equipment until we had a good home batch coffee roasting set up. It was at this point that PF roasting, which was still owned by my dad, was converted from a food service company to a coffee roasting company.

This information is mostly true, except for the part about PF roasting becoming a coffee roasting company.
